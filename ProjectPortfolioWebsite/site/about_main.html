About Mitchell Oleson

Who am I?

Adventurer

Thrilled by the unknown, I am always seeking a new challenge and actively engaging new fields. I love the wilderness and have been on numerous backpacking and camping trips in the Southern Rockies. Recently, I have begun exploring the national parks, starting with backpacking in Zion and Bryce Canyon over spring break.

Engineer

Hands on technical challenges fascinate me. I invest hours in my projects to ensure the final system is reliable and robust. This was paramount in the numerous tweaks, retries, and revisions of my Hyper-V server. Addtionally, in pursuit of new challenges, I joined Berkeley Hyperloop (bLoop)as Signals and Controls lead and am President of Cal AIAA as well as the project lead for the Robo-Ops Competition Team (under Cal AIAA).

Scholar

I love school for the challenges it presents and the vast amounts of information it exposes me to. In my opinion, grades come second to learning with my first three years at Berkeley a mad rush. Since starting, I have always been enrolled in more than 18 units (5-6 classes), a brother at Pi Kappa Phi fraternity, a competition team memeber for BERKE1337 (a cybersecurity club), President of Cal AIAA, as well as a team lead for both Cal Rover and Berkeley Hyperloop (bLoop).

Enthusiast

System building has always been one of my hobbies and to date I have designed, purchased and built from scratch two desktop computers. One of which currently powers a videowall running 12 49" TV's for the media center at my former High School. The other has gone through rigourous overclocking and experimentation under my curious hands and currently runs my home file server and this website via a Debian server installation through Windows Hyper-V

Key Attributes

While working with people on Cal Rover and screening people for bLoop I have come to admire and actively seek out the following characteristics:

  • Reliable
  • Motivated
  • Independent
  • Hard Worker
  • Honest
  • Trustworthy

People with these characteristics have demonstrated that when given a task they will get it done on time and to the best of their ability. There is no need to heckle or constantly watch over their shoulder. Once they have a good idea of what needs to be they can utilize the resources at their disposal and get the task done.
