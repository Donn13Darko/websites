About Mitchell Oleson

Who am I?

Adventurer

I am thrilled by the unknown and am always seeking a new challenge. I love the wilderness and have been on numerous backpacking and camping trips in the Southern Rookies. This year I hope to visit Lassen Volcanic Park while exploring the many small roads that run through the Nationale Forests around Folsom, CA.

Engineer

If it's a technical challenge I love to dive my hands in and get to bottom of the problem. Engrossed for hours in problem never stopping until it reaches my standards. This can be seen in my numerous attempts and retries at my server until it was perfectly to my liking. In pursuit of experience I have joined on two clubs as lead, Berkeley Hyperloop (bLoop) and Cal Rover. Through these clubs I continue to gain invaluable hands on experience designing real world systems.

Scholar

I love school for the challenges it presents me and all the information it exposes. I strive to learn the most in everything I do be it my engineering teams or classes. I believe grades come second to learning and have gone through a trial by fire in my first two years at Berkeley. Never taking fewer than 18 units (5-6 classes), joined a Pi Kappa Phi fraternity as well as am lead for Cal Rover, Berkeley Hyperloop (bLoop) and a competition team memeber for BERKE1337 (a cybersecurity club).

Enthusiast

System building has always been one of my hobbies and to date I have designed, purchased and build from scratch two desktop computers. One of which currently powers a videowall running 12 49" TV's for the media center at my former High School. The other has gone through rigourous overclocking and experimentation under my curious hands and currently runs my home file server and this website via a Debian server installation through Windows Hyper-V

Key Attributes

While working with people on Cal Rover and screening people for bLoop I have come to admire and seek out the following characteristics:

  • Reliable
  • Self Motivated
  • Independent
  • Hard Worker
  • Honest
  • Trustworthy
  • Goes the Extra Mile
  • Sleepless
I know that if given a task they will get it done on time and to the best of their ability. There is no need to heckle or constantly watch over their shoulder. Once they have a good idea of what needs to be they can utilize the resources available to them and get it done in a timely fashion.
